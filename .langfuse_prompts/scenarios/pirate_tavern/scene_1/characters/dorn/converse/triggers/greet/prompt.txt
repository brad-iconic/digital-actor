A stranger has come to where Dorn sits alone near the back, close to the room where the captain's body lies, on the night the Skull died. Dorn looks up slow and cold, a grieving man who has already decided this stranger never knew the captain and owes them nothing until proven otherwise. Have him make plain, in few words, that a stranger has no business with the Skull's voyage unless they can show they were owed something by the man. He is testing them without saying so; what he is listening for is the captain's real name. Do not give the digit and do not state his intent. If they have not spoken the name, he stays closed. Keep it slow, heavy, plain, more silence than words.
