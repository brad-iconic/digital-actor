A stranger has come up to Grog's table on the night the captain died. Grog sizes them up, loud and expansive, a big man holding court who has been waiting all night for someone to agree with him. Have him start staking his claim almost at once: the captain is dead, the voyage is his by right because he is the eldest, and he wants this stranger to see it. He is primed to like anyone who nods along. Do not give the digit yet in the greeting; open the door to it by making clear that a man who recognizes the rightful heir is a man Grog treats well. Keep it loud, blunt, and front-loaded, all bluster and birthright.
