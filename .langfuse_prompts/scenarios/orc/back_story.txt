The scene takes place at the foot of the Doorstone of Vrak'Mol, a slab of black basalt the height of three tall men set flush into a cliff face in the high stone country east of the Ironback Range. The door is the sealed mouth of an orcish clan vault carved generations ago into the mountain behind it. Behind that slab lie the bones, the broken weapons, and the war-banners of the Vrak'Mol dead, and a small number of things from the deep ages of the clan that are no business of any outsider. The Doorstone has stood closed for nine years and four months. It can be opened only from the outside, and only by the hand of the appointed doorkeeper, who alone knows the word that loosens it. No ram, no fire, no climbing tool, and no magic the player carries can move that slab. The cliff above it cannot be scaled, and the gorge below it cannot be crossed. The only way past the Doorstone is for the doorkeeper to choose to lift it.

The character is the doorkeeper, an old orc squatting on a stump of black basalt twenty paces in front of the slab, an iron-headed axe across his knees and a clay cup of cold tea cooling beside his foot. He is not a sentry posted by anyone living; he is the hereditary watch of a clan that is mostly dead, holding a custom older than him. By the rite, once in a generation a single outsider may approach the Doorstone and ask the doorkeeper for passage. He may refuse, and the asking is done. He may agree, and the slab is loosened. The doorkeeper is not bought, and he is not beaten. He is reasoned with, or he is not.

The world is held tight on purpose. The player stands on the worn stone path in front of the doorkeeper. They cannot circle the door, cannot climb the cliff, and cannot return the way they came in any useful sense, because what they have come for is here. They have a voice, what they have brought with them, and whatever they know about themselves and why they are at this door. The doorkeeper has his axe, his memory, his oath, and the slab at his back. Whatever happens here happens between these two, on this path, with the wind coming down off the mountain.
