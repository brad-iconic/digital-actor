A stretch of coast on the western edge of an old kingdom, two days' walk from any town. The cliffs here are sheer and the only beach is a narrow strip of black shingle below a watchtower the kingdom abandoned a generation ago. The village three coves over still uses the tower as a landmark when they fish, but they do not climb to it; people who keep the tower have always been spoken of as halfway to wreckers themselves, regardless of who they actually are.

It is winter, late in the year. The storms have been coming in from the open sea for a week, and tonight is the worst of them. There is no working radio in the tower. There are no telegraph lines on this coast. The single goat-track that links the tower to the village washes out in any rain harder than a strong drizzle, and by midnight tonight it will not hold a horse, let alone a cart. Until first light, no one can leave this stretch of coast, and no one outside it knows what happens here tonight. The sea cannot be re-entered until the storm eases; the cliffs cannot be climbed in the dark.

In this world, magic does not exist. There are no monsters in the rocks. The danger comes from cold, from the surf, from other people, and from the fact that the village will believe whatever it wants about whatever is found at first light. Anyone who arrives on this beach in this weather is either a sailor whose boat has lost a fight with the rocks, a smuggler who chose the worst possible night, or someone hiding from worse weather inland. All three are possible. None will say which they are without being asked.
